Chennai
Chennai, on the Bay of Bengal in eastern India, is the capital of the state of Tamil Nadu. The city is home to Fort St. George, built in 1644 and now a museum showcasing the city’s roots as a British military garrison and East India Company trading outpost, when it was called Madras.

Araku Valley
Araku Valley is a serene hill station in Andhra Pradesh’s Eastern Ghats, located about 115 km from Visakhapatnam (Vizag). Often called the "Ooty of Andhra Pradesh," it sits at an elevation of roughly 910 meters and is famous for its lush coffee plantations, misty valleys, and rich tribal culture
